Hailing from Pattaya, Thailand, Tawanchai is easily one of the best Muay Thai Fighters in the world. With his lightning-fast, thundering kicks, and phenomenal skills in the clinch, he is a force to be reckoned with. He currently trains at the legendary PK Saenchai Muay Thai Gym in Bangkok and has become famous world wide not only in the Muay Thai scene, but also the kickboxing scene.

After watching some Muay Thai fights in Pattaya, Thailand, when he was 7 years old, Tawanchai PK Saenchai was inspired to learn ‚Äúthe art of eight limbs‚Äù and quickly found himself competing in the sport.

He tore up the local scene and eventually made his debut at the historic Lumpinee Boxing Stadium when he was 14. In 2018, Tawanchai won the Lumpinee Boxing Stadium, Sports Authority of Thailand, and Siam Kela Fighter of the Year awards.

Just three years later, Tawanchai joined ONE in search of his first World Title.

The Pattaya native made a splash in his promotional debut when he knocked out Sean Clancy with a head kick. He then fought multiple-time Kickboxing World Champion Sitthichai Sitsongpeenong in a bout for the ages.

But after he knocked out his close friend, Saemapetch Fairtex, in the first round of their catchweight bout in January 2022, Tawanchai realized he had trouble making the bantamweight limit. That prompted him to move up to featherweight.

In his divisional debut, Tawanchai displayed his firebrand style of fighting. He finished Niclas Larsen with a crisp two-punch combination in the second round.

That dominant win propelled Tawanchai to the biggest opportunity of his career ‚Äì a shot at longtime ONE Featherweight Muay Thai World Champion Petchmorakot Petchyindee, who he dethroned for the belt at ONE 161.

He then fought against a fellow Muay Thai Champion, Superbon Singha Mawynn, which ended in a majority decision for Tawanchai, successfully defending his title as the Featherweight Muay Thai champion of the world.
